The Verdict

Anarchy v Order: {{_attributes.alignmentFlag('freedomVOrder')}}

Anarchy
Order

You revel in your ability to resist the status quo. Whenever you have the option to sow the seeds of chaos, you eagerly disobey commands for the simple reason of seeing the result of your dissention. You place faith in your own abilities and do not depend on the guidance of others to govern your actions. This may or may not cause you to trample on the good will and freedom of others. At best you champion the rights of all. At worst, you care only for your own agenda.

Example Perk: The anarchy alignment allows you to take actions that benefit you greatly; sometimes at the expense of others. You can also make unorthodox decisions in situations where moral judgement is necessary.

As a neutral individual, you understand the nature of balance between both freedom and order. True freedom is anarchy and leads to destruction, yet oppression by its very nature does not serve the greater good. You make decisions in this grey area between competing forces. Or maybe you just have difficulty making up your mind? We will see which sort of individual you are in the future.

Example Perk: The neutral alignment allows you to see both sides of an argument. You are particularly adept at avoiding confrontation. While you benefit from a greater freedom of choices, you will be unable to choose certain alignment-specific choices in some situations.

You value the security of order over individual freedom. Its not that you necessarily crave power itself, though that may be the case, you simply dislike the chaos brought about by change or free will. Even if some rules seem inane, it is better to follow them since they are likely in place for a reason. Because of your preference for the status quo, you are more likely to adhere strictly to your ethos and judge dissenters harshly. Thanks to your strong morale compass, you can act quickly in situations that would give others pause.

Example Perk: The order alignment grants you a firm understanding of social norms allowing you to make the most "appropriate" decision in certain situations.

Nihil v Faith: {{_attributes.alignmentFlag('nihilVFaith')}}

Nihil
Faith

People act according to their own interests, so you are constantly on the lookout for hidden agendas. The concepts of good and evil only exist to govern the individual. This does not mean that you lack an empathy for others, yet you rarely act on such feelings due to your distrustful nature. You heavily favor the concept of risk v reward when making your decisions to approach the problems you face over placing faith in individuals.

Example Perk: Some call it paranoia, but you have a heightened sense of the "true" nature of people. The nihil alignment grants you the ability to gauge the hostility of others and can detect malicious intent when others may not.

The neutral individual may believe the best in others, but does not always extend a hand whenever they see someone in need. Maybe you help individuals on a risk v reward basis or maybe you have no rhyme or reason behind the decisions you make. Some people just feel worth saving while others are undeserving. Regardless of why you make your decisions, you seem to operate on a sliding scale based on your sense of empathy and an individual's perceived level of need.

Example Perk: The neutral alignment can freely choose to help individuals as they see fit. You will be able to approach most situations rationally, but will lack the faith to help everyone you see.

Though people may falter, they are ultimately good. You feel that everyone may be redeemed regardless of how low they may sink. Whether it is due to the faith you have in humanity, or your faith in a higher power, you shine as a beacon of hope in a world filled with inequity and injustice. Or maybe you're just naive?

Example Perk: The faith alignment grants you the ability to extend a helping hand to individuals in nearly any situation. Though danger may abound, you can look past the risks and help those in need. This may or may not backfire in certain situations.
