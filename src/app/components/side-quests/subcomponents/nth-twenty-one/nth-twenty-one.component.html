At the 21st Hour

Traveling along the roadside, you find a gathering of people surrounding a well-dressed man sitting casually in a chair counting what appears to be his latest winnings. His flamboyant attire suggests that he is somewhat of an entertainer, or at the very least, a gentleman that that enjoys partaking in games of chance.

As the buzz of the awed audience reaches your ear, you lock eyes with the gentleman as he waves you over with a sly smile. Curious, you decide to investigate what all the commotion is about. This could be an opportunity to gather information and dabble in a bit of trade with the crowd at the very least.

"Greetings traveler! You seem like the enterprising sort, my name is Harvey Harlington. I enjoy a wager here and there, and I'm sure an intelligent gentleman such as yourself surely prefers a game of wits. Well sir, may I interest you in a game of 21?"

"Well, that is a shame good sir. I bid you good day."

A bit disappointed, Harvey sits back down in his chair and resumes his banter with his audience. You decide to move along and continue with your travels.

"Excellent! And your name good sir? Lupin! Well Lupin, pull up a chair and I'll explain the rules of the game."

You grab the chair opposite of Harvey and sit down. You're sure that this man does not play with a full deck of cards. Whatever game you play, he will likely try to take advantage of you in some manner.

"Well then, the game is simple. We start this here counter at 0. We each take turns increasing the number anywhere from 1 to 3 times each. We go back and forth in this manner until the number reaches 21. Whomever increases the counter to 21 will be the loser. Simple enough, right?"

"Now, being the magnanimous man I am, I shall even let you go first since you're new to this game."

You gleam a sense of disgust wash over Harvey's face, but only for a moment. With the same sly smile that he greeted you with, Harvey responds in kind.

"Surely you would not waste the chance to gain the advantage in the match? I offered you this handicap out of goodwill. Will you not allow yourself to gain the upperhand?"

Harvey begins to lose his composure.

"Very well Lupin! Do not cry when I mop the floor with you. I tried to do the gentlemanly thing and let you go first. Now, go ahead and make your move. Whatever it is, I am sure it will end in your defeat."

Harvey listlessly stares at the number 20 without saying a word. It would appear that he did not expect you to see through his deception so completely. It would seem that lady luck has four-saken him in his time of need.

You gather your earnings, tip your cap, and head back to the main road to continue your journey.

Staring at the number 20 on the counter, you realize your inevitable defeat.

"Sorry ole bean, I'm afraid you never really had a chance. Tough break."
