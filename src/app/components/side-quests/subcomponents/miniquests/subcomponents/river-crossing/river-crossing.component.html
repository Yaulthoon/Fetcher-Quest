River Crossing

Continuing on the path you laid out, you come to the bridge that you must travel over only to find that construction workers have blocked off the bridge. They insist that no one can travel across until maintenance is complete. Inquiring about the length of the delay, you realize that waiting for the workers to be done will cost you distance that you were expecting to travel today.

Looking for alternatives, you see a small riverboat nearby. Speaking with the owner of the small boat, he offers to take you across the river to the otherside for 50 gold.

Alternatively, there seems to be enough timber around to make a small raft yourself if you find that you are impatient. Who knows how long this effort would take or if the raft will even make it across safely.

You cannot help but feel that you have seen this situation before. You realize that waiting for the workers will cost you half of your distance for the day. Paying the riverboat captain will allow you to lose no progress and only cost the 50 gold. Building your own raft at best would let you keep about 75% of your expected progress, but capsizing could cost you anywhere from 2 to 5 supplies were you to fail.

With your abilities as a Tumblemancer, you are sure you could quickly make a raft in no time that would safely ferry you across. As long as you concentrate on your spell, there would be no danger involved at all.

Mulling over the choices, you ultimately decide to:

Even though it annoys you to be held up by the work going on, there is no need to spend excessively or risk injury just to make up some time. With plenty of supplies, you do not feel pressed into making a risky decision. After some time, they finish and you can resume your journey. You end up losing about half your expected distance, but you knew the price of waiting beforehand, so this comes as little surprise.

You pay the riverboat captain the 50 gold to travel across the river. Without incident, you make your way to the other side of the river and continue on your way. Hustling a little more to make up some lost time, you were able to prevent any loss in progress on the day's travel. Sometimes it pays to spend a little gold to save your return on investment.

Through your tumblemancy, you weave a fine raft in no time and easily cross the river to the other side. In fact, you were so quick with your efforts that you lost no time on the day's progress. The construction workers can only look on in awe as you renew your travel along the road to your destination.

Even though you surely lost some progress on the day's travel, building the raft turned out to be a worthwhile gamble. You cross the river without any issue. Deciding to leave the raft behind for anyone else who may be able to use it, you move on no worse for wear.

After finishing your raft, you can tell it looks a little shaky, but with such a short distance to the otherside of the river, you feel its still worth the risk. As you begin to cross, you almost immediately begin to take on water! Although you hurry across the rest of the way, your wagon takes on water as a result of your raft sinking.

Luckily, the raft made it far enough that the water is shallow enough to pull your wagon the rest of the way out of the water. While you do end up saving a majority of the day's travel with your raft gamble, you wonder if the extra loss in supplies was worth it or not.
